The daily worry
"I'm 800 km away and I don't know if Appa took his medicine today."

Parents grow older in one city while their children build lives in another. A missed BP or sugar tablet isn't a small thing — but calling every day to check feels like nagging, and most of the time you just… don't know.

How it works

Set it up once. Then it just runs.

You do the thinking. Your parent does one tap. You only hear from us when it matters.

You add their medicines

Snap a photo of the prescription — we read it and set up the schedule. Doses, timings, refills.

They get a WhatsApp reminder

A gentle message at each dose time, in language they're comfortable with. They reply "Taken" — that's it.

You're told only if it's missed

Confirmed doses log quietly. Miss one, and you get a nudge — so a bad day never goes unnoticed.

Why WhatsApp

Nothing for your parent to install.

Every other app asks your parent to download, sign up, and learn a new thing. Most won't. CareLink meets them where they already are — the WhatsApp they use every day.

  • ✓ Reminders arrive on WhatsApp — no new app for them
  • ✓ Reminders in their own language
  • ✓ Your parent always sees who's helping them, and can stop anytime
  • ✓ You see whether doses were taken — nothing else. No tracking.
